About Me


My name is Rohit Musti and I am an graduate student at the University of Virginia studying Computer Science. I love computer science for the puzzles it offers, but often myself at odds with the culture of exclusion that often surrounds it. I am passioned about and have worked hard to make computing more inclusive through my student taught Spatial Reasoning class. I believe knowledge and understanding of computing is essential for any young person growing up today to be able to fully understand and interact with systems of power. I hope by increasing the diversity of people with an understanding of computing, we will have more perspectives at the table and more opportunities to hold power accountable.

Currently, I am working on the courts data project. This is part of an effort to make the US court system accessible to the broader public and more accountable to the people. It is my second major civic technology project. My first project was co-creating and teaching the Digital Governance Lab. The goal of the class was to educate policy makers about computing and computing folks about policy work. I am excited to be leading the courts data project and hope it is the first step in a career dedicated to the public interest.
