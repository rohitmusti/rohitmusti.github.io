CS1501: Spatial Reasoning

I created this website as a tool I used in teaching a class on spatial reasoning that I designed. Spatial reasoning skills are strongly correlated with success in engineering and computer science, especially at the introductory levels. Sometimes this is due to the spatial nature of the tasks, but more often it is because professors, texts, and peers use diagrams, figures, and spatial terms and ideas to help explain complicated but not intrinsically spatial concepts. Thus, many students, who didn’t develop these skills before coming to college, are at a disadvantage. This course, CS1501: Spatial Reasoning, is a student-taught class, geared towards students who are interested in computer science or engineering, or thinking of taking a class on a related subject during their time at UVa.

Auto-Diagram Generation

This project is narrow in application, but powerful nonetheless. Red Hat's Open Innovation Labs creates a lot of infrastructure through automation. With this automated infrastructure, they need automated documentation, this includes diagram. As part of my summer 2018 internship for Red Hat, I built out this tool from scratch which (1) takes in a JSON file, (2) uses the data to create a diagram using VueJS, (3) spins up a node server, (4) takes a screenshot of the application using headless chrome, (5) serves up a png of the screenshot through an API, and (6) is entirely automated using Ansible.

At the Disrupt the District Hackathon, the team I was a part of built an Android app that awards points based on how little the phone is used. It awards 1 point for every minute the phone is locked.
